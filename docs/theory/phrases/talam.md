<h2> Talam </h2>

<p> Talam is a sequence of beats used to indicate the passage of <a href = "{{site.baseurl}}/docs/theory/phrases/matra/">Matras</a>.</p>

<p>
The sequence of beats usually have a structure, and they are periodic. The Matra contained within a period, and also between succesive beats are predetermined. These two features unqiuely determine a Talam.
</p>

<p>
In Carnatic Music, Talams are organized into a neat structure called the <b> Sapta Tala Pattika </b> (Seven Tala Table). Before delving into the table, some notations for putting the beats  are introduced.
</p>

<ul>
    <li> 1 - <b>Lakhu</b> - One forehand clap + counting of fingers </li>
    <li> 0 - <b>Dhrutham</b> - One forehand clap + One backhand clap </li>
    <li> U - <b>AnuDhrutham</b> - One forehand clap </li>

</ul>
<p>
In Lakhu (1), when we mention counting of fingers, the number of fingers to be counted is specified using <b> Jathi </b> (not the dance one).
</p>
